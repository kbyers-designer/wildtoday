North Carolina Conservancy Acquires 800-Acre Neuse Islands Wilderness

The Triangle Land Conservancy has purchased the Neuse Islands Wilderness, an 800-acre preserve of swamp cypress and hardwood forests along the Neuse River in Johnston County, North Carolina, for $4.6 million. The acquisition, supported by Johnston County, the state, and the U.S. Air Force, protects critical wildlife habitat including oxbow lakes, native rivercane grasses, and trees over 200 years old. The land also contains a historic Black cemetery and Native American archaeological sites.

West Virginia Protects 170+ Miles of Native Brook Trout Streams

West Virginia has newly designated more than 170 miles of mountain streams as protected trout waters following field surveys using environmental DNA sampling and electrofishing. The expansion protects naturally reproducing native brook trout, the state fish and only trout species native to Appalachia. A partnership of Trout Unlimited, the West Virginia Rivers Coalition, and state agencies confirmed these streams provide exceptional habitat for aquatic insects, salamanders, and other wildlife.

3
Ladakh Deploys 100 Ex-Servicemen to Environmental Protection Force

In a first-of-its-kind initiative, Ladakh's Lieutenant Governor Vinai Kumar Saxena deployed 100 ex-servicemen into the Ladakh Environment Protection Force to protect the region's fragile high-altitude ecosystem. The force will patrol ecologically sensitive areas to prevent illegal off-roading, unauthorized camping, wildlife disturbance, and pollution. Members are authorized to issue on-the-spot fines and will receive monthly compensation while monitoring their native areas.

4
Woodside Energy Invests $5 Million in Louisiana Wetland Restoration

Woodside Energy is investing US$5 million to restore threatened habitats in Sam Houston Jones State Park in Louisiana over the next six years. The project, the largest corporate donation ever received by a Louisiana state park, will remove invasive species, plant approximately 200,000 native trees, and restore prescribed fire regimes across over 800 acres. Restoration will protect longleaf pine forests, diverse wetlands, bald eagles, and native bat populations.

5
Australia: Community Seeks National Park Expansion to Protect Greater Gliders

The Gerroa Environment Protection Society is campaigning to expand Seven Mile Beach National Park in New South Wales to protect the Greater Glider and 12 other endangered species. The proposed expansion would add 450 hectares to the current national park, providing the full 1,000 hectares experts say is needed for a thriving glider population. The effort comes as 2019 bushfires decimated glider populations in other protected areas.

6
Deforestation Impacts on Worker Health in Tropical Regions

A study published in One Earth found that deforestation and climate change combined have made outdoor work unsafe for millions in tropical regions. Between 2003 and 2018, approximately 4.9 million people lost at least half an hour per day of safe working temperatures. The research shows that deforestation's local warming effects—caused by loss of tree shade and reduced evapotranspiration—exceed immediate climate change impacts in some areas.

7
Desert Bighorn Sheep Relocated from Nevada's Valley of Fire

The Nevada Department of Wildlife relocated 39 desert bighorn sheep from Valley of Fire State Park to address food and water shortages caused by drought. The capture and translocation operation aims to ease pressure on the herd during the challenging environmental conditions in the park, with relocation helping the animals find better forage and water sources.

8
Brazil's Amazon Deforestation Continues to Decline

Brazil's efforts to combat Amazon deforestation are showing measurable results, with deforestation falling significantly during the Southern Hemisphere's wet season (August-March). While habitat loss and human activity continue to pressure these ecosystems, the declining rates represent progress in large-scale forest protection efforts backed by coordinated enforcement and community partnerships.
